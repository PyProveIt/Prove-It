This theorem is very similar to its companion theorem pos_domain_in_full_domain_sans_zero and the approach is essentially the same.
The basic approach is to show that
$\ell\in\{-2^{t-1}+1 \ldots -(\epsilon+1)\}$ means $\ell\in\{-2^{t-1}+1\ldots 2^{t-1}\}$ and $\ell\ne 0$
More specifically:
(1) $\ell\in\{-2^{t-1}+1 \ldots -(\epsilon+1)\}$ means $\ell \ge -2^{t-1}+1$ (and $\ell\in\mathbb{Z}$)
(2) $\ell\in\{\epsilon+1\ldots 2^{t-1}\}$ means $\ell \le -(\epsilon+1)$
(3) $\epsilon\in\{1\ldots 2^{t-1}-2\}$ means $-(\epsilon + 1) < 0$
(4) Thus $-2^{t-1 + 1} \le \ell \le 2^{t-1}$ and $\ell<0$.
(5) Thus $-2^{t-1 + 1} \le \ell \le 2^{t-1}$ and $\ell\ne 0$.
(6) Thus $\ell\in\{-2^{t-1 + 1}\ldots 2^{t-1}\}-\{0\}$
