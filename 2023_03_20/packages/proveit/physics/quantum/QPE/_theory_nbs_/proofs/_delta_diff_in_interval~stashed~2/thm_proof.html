Under these presumptions, we begin our proof of
_delta_diff_in_interval:
(see dependencies)

One possibility:
We know $2^t \delta \in [0, 1)$, so we could look at $2^t \delta - \ell$, for $\ell \in \{-2^{t-1}+1, \ldots, 2^{t-1}\}$.
Then $2^t \delta - 2^{t-1} \le 2^t \delta - \ell \le 2^t \delta + (2^{t-1}-1)$.
Then $0 - 2^{t-1} \le 2^t \delta - \ell < 1 + (2^{t-1}-1)$.
Then $- 2^{t-1} \le 2^t \delta - \ell < 2^{t-1}$.
Dividing everything by $2^{t}$ then gives:
$-\frac{2^{t-1}}{2^t} \le \delta - \frac{\ell}{2^t} < \frac{2^{t-1}}{2^t}$,
which further simplifies to:
$- \frac{1}{2} \le \delta - \frac{\ell}{2^t} < \frac{1}{2}$
